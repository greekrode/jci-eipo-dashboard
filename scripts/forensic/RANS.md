## Thesis

RANS (PT RANS Entertainmen Indonesia Tbk) is a celebrity-IP media holding company built around Raffi Ahmad and Nagita Slavina, raising up to Rp429.25bn for 20.02% in a 100% primary offering. The bull case is a genuine, hard-to-replicate brand moat (one of Indonesia's largest social-media audiences) attached to a clean, almost debt-free balance sheet, with proceeds aimed at growth rather than repair. The bear case is in the trailing numbers: revenue has declined three years running and net profit fell 42% in 2025, while the deal is priced at a demanding ~28-35x earnings. This is a bet that the brand can re-accelerate a softening top line, paid for at a premium multiple, with the entire equity story resting on two people.

## How the business works

RANS monetises the audience and brand of Raffi Ahmad, Nagita Slavina and family across two audited segments. The Hiburan & Lifestyle segment (about 70% of FY2025 revenue and the profit engine, at roughly 35% segment margin) earns from social-media content and advertising, brand-ambassador and endorsement deals, talent management, a production house, and live events and ticketing. The Penjualan Produk segment (about 30%) sells IP-branded food, beverage and beauty products, and was loss-making in 2025 (a segment loss of around Rp12bn). The defining dependency is key-person concentration: revenue and brand value are tied to the continued involvement and reputation of Raffi and Nagita, which the issuer names as its own number-one risk. The demand backdrop is a large, young, social-first Indonesian consumer base, but the asset being sold is fundamentally the reach and goodwill of a single family.

## The offering & ownership

The IPO is 2,525,000,000 new shares (20.02% of post-IPO capital), entirely primary, so all proceeds accrue to the company. Pricing is Rp135 to Rp170 (par Rp10), implying a post-IPO market capitalisation of roughly Rp1.70tn to Rp2.14tn on 12,609,250,000 shares, underwritten on a full-commitment basis by Trimegah. Ownership is concentrated: Raffi Farid Ahmad holds 62.93% post-IPO as sole controller and ultimate beneficial owner, with the founder and family bloc (nine pre-IPO holders including Nagita Slavina, Dony Oskaria, Soultan Ariq Rachman and Kaesang Pangarep) at 79.98% against a 20.02% public float. The lock-up is genuinely strong: all nine pre-IPO holders signed 8-month statutory POJK 25/2017 lock-up statements, and Raffi has separately committed to retain control for three years. There is no ESA or MESOP programme. Dividend policy is up to 35% of net profit starting FY2026.

## Governance & related parties

Control and economic ownership are aligned in one person, which removes the control-mismatch question seen in some peers but raises the opposite concern: minority influence is minimal under a single ~63% controller. The use of proceeds carries the related-party item to watch. About 19.8% of the raise (Rp85bn) funds an acquisition tied to the Slavina/family brand, and a further slice funds an AI joint venture (Feedloop) and the Cipungland project, so a meaningful portion of public money flows into ventures within the founder's orbit; the arm's-length basis and independent valuation of these should be confirmed in the final prospectus. The board's independent-commissioner substance and the related-party framework around content and endorsement deals (where the controller is also the product) are the governance areas a careful reader should weigh. The issuer reports a clean legal record.

## Reading the financials

The trajectory is the crux. Revenue declined each of the last three years, from Rp437.8bn (2023) to Rp410.5bn (2024) to Rp353.4bn (2025). Reported net profit looks like it rose then fell sharply, but the 2024 figure is flattered: it includes a one-off Rp44.9bn gain on the disposal of a subsidiary (the RPKSB sports business), offset by a Rp10.5bn loss from that discontinued operation, leaving total profit for the year of Rp97.1bn. Strip the disposal out and the underlying earnings trend is softening rather than growing; 2025 total net profit of Rp56.7bn is down about 42%. Note that profit attributable to parent (Rp61.0bn in 2025) exceeds total profit because non-controlling interests absorbed losses, so valuation should use the parent figure. The balance sheet, by contrast, is a real strength: total liabilities of Rp120.2bn against equity of Rp340.8bn put DER at just 0.35x (down from 0.62x in 2023), interest cover is roughly 20x, and the only interest-bearing debt is a single BNI investment facility (about Rp37bn) that the IPO largely retires. This is a low-leverage, cash-generative business; the question is top-line direction, not solvency.

## Valuation

At the range, RANS prices at roughly 27.9 to 35.1x trailing FY2025 earnings attributable to parent (about 30 to 38x on total profit), with post-money price-to-book near 2.3 to 2.8x. That is a demanding multiple to pay for a company whose revenue has fallen three years running and whose 2024 profit was inflated by a non-recurring disposal. The multiple only makes sense if the brand-led growth plan (concerts, the Slavina acquisition, IP products, the AI venture) genuinely reignites the top line and the Product segment turns. Pre-money ROE of 16.6% dilutes to roughly 8% post-raise as the equity base expands. Note that issuance costs and the proforma equity table are blank in this preliminary draft, so net proceeds and exact post-money book value are back-solved and should be confirmed. A relative read needs live multiples for Indonesian media and IP peers at publication.

## Bottom line

RANS pairs a rare asset, a durable celebrity-IP brand on a clean, low-leverage balance sheet, with an awkward financial profile and a full price. The 100% primary, growth-oriented structure and the strong lock-up are points in its favour, and the deleveraging is almost incidental because there is little debt to repay. Against that sit three years of declining revenue, a 42% profit drop once the 2024 disposal gain is set aside, a loss-making product arm, related-party uses of proceeds, and a ~28-35x multiple. The entire thesis reduces to one question: can the Raffi and Nagita brand convert reach into re-accelerating, recurring revenue, and is that worth paying a premium for while the trailing line still points down. Underwrite it only if you believe the brand re-rates the growth, not the history.
